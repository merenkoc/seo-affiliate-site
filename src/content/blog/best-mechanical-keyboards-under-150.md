## What to Look For
When choosing the best mechanical keyboard under $150, there are several factors to consider. Here are some key buying criteria to keep in mind:
* **Switch type**: Look for keyboards with high-quality switches, such as Cherry MX or Romer-G switches.
* **Customizable lighting**: Consider keyboards with customizable lighting options, such as RGB lighting or backlighting.
* **Durability**: Look for keyboards with durable designs and materials, such as aluminum frames or ergonomic designs.
* **Wireless connectivity**: Consider keyboards with wireless connectivity options, such as Bluetooth or 2.4GHz wireless.
* **Price**: Set a budget and look for keyboards that offer great value for the price.

## FAQ
Q: What is the best mechanical keyboard switch type?
A: The best mechanical keyboard switch type depends on personal preference, but Cherry MX and Romer-G switches are popular options.
Q: Are wireless mechanical keyboards reliable?
A: Yes, wireless mechanical keyboards can be reliable, but some users may experience issues with connectivity or interference.
Q: What is the difference between a tenkeyless and full-size keyboard?
A: A tenkeyless keyboard is a compact keyboard that lacks a numpad, while a full-size keyboard includes a numpad and is generally larger.
Q: Can I customize the lighting on my mechanical keyboard?
A: Yes, many mechanical keyboards offer customizable lighting options, such as RGB lighting or backlighting.
Q: Are budget-friendly mechanical keyboards worth it?
A: Yes, budget-friendly mechanical keyboards can offer great value and performance, but may lack some features or have lower-quality switches.
