## In-Depth Reviews

Our verdict: The Nikon D5600 is a great value for its price, offering excellent image quality and features.

### Canon EOS Rebel T8i
The Canon EOS Rebel T8i features a 24.1MP APS-C sensor, 4K video, and a vari-angle touchscreen. It is an ideal choice for beginners and professionals alike, offering a great balance of features and price. Users love the camera's 4K video capabilities, with one user stating, "The vari-angle touchscreen is also super useful for vlogging." However, some users have reported issues with the camera's autofocus in certain lighting conditions. Our verdict: The Canon EOS Rebel T8i is a great choice for those who want a camera with 4K video capabilities and a vari-angle touchscreen.

### Sony Alpha a6400
The Sony Alpha a6400 features a 24.2MP Exmor CMOS sensor, 4K video, and fast autofocus. It is an excellent choice for professionals and videographers, offering high-quality images and video in a compact body. Users love the camera's autofocus, with one user stating, "The autofocus is lightning-fast and the image quality is superb." However, some users have reported issues with the camera's menu system, citing that it can be confusing for beginners. Our verdict: The Sony Alpha a6400 is an incredible camera for its price, offering excellent autofocus, image quality, and compact design.

## What to Look For
When choosing the best professional camera under $2,000, there are several factors to consider. Here are three key buying criteria to keep in mind:
* Sensor size and type: A full-frame sensor offers better image quality and low-light performance, but may be more expensive.
* Video capabilities: If you plan to shoot video, look for a camera with 4K resolution and a vari-angle touchscreen.
* Autofocus: Fast and accurate autofocus is essential for capturing sharp images, especially in low-light conditions.
